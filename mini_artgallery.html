The Dark Arts Of Henrik Uldalen("Infractus")

It's clear that London-based Norwegian artist, Henrik Uldalen deals in figurative paintings (and very good ones at that), but they're updated with a contemporary disposition. Dark, ethereal, sublime, otherworldly and other such words spring to mind on first viewing his work. The pieces have a hypnotizing and bewitching quality, and this seems to have become somewhat of a signature for Uldalen. Impressively, he's a talented self-taught artist and actually describes himself as an "expressionist painter trapped in the body of a neoclassicist painter" so there are a few influences at work here, but the intermix is pure magic.
Tell us about your background...

I'm a Norwegian, adopted from South Korea when I was five months old. I grew up in a small town named Asker, just outside of Oslo, Norway. In these last ten years I have been traveling and living in different countries, finally to settle down in London, UK, where I have my studio, gallery and life. I have been working with paintings full time since around 2008, after finishing my education to be a primary school teacher. I started painting whilst studying, and went straight on to showing in galleries after the education ended. I, therefore, have no formal art education.
